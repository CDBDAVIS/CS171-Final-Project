Building a better restaurant

Yelp is a powerful tool for the consumer — providing reviews, menus, photos, and other information about restaurants — all valuable information in deciding where to eat. But for the restaurant owner, the stakes are much higher. Every customer that decides to eat somewhere else is a direct hit to a restaurant’s profits. Luckily, Yelp — with its millions of customer reviews — can be a valuable tool for boosting a restaurant’s foot traffic, longevity, and profitability… if you know what to look for. We have curated Yelp data from 9 North American metropolitan areas to find out just how to optimize a restaurant.

Is anyone still using Yelp? Didn’t it come out nearly 20 years ago?

Astute in observing that Yelp is older than smartphones! While Yelp was officially launched in 2004, reviews continue to pour in 16 years later. As the visualization shows, Yelp’s review-count isn’t just still increasing; the actual rate at which Yelp’s review count is growing is increasing too! This means that more and more reviews are written each day. From users in our representative 9 cities alone, Yelp received more than 8,000,000 reviews. If each Yelp user only wrote one review, that is equivalent to 8 out of every 11 people across the entire population of those 9 cities writing a review.

So why should you care what strangers on the internet think about your restaurant?

Ever heard the proverb success begets success? The higher your restaurant’s ratings are on Yelp, the more likely Yelp’s loyal followers are to flock to your restaurant — bestowing more business and more “check-ins”, the metric Yelp uses to track when users visit a restaurant. Restaurants with higher star ratings, especially those with 4 stars and above, see an increased number of check-ins. More check-ins, more business, more success. As such, a restaurant’s “star-power” on Yelp is a meaningful indicator of success. Hover over an individual restaurant's dot to see additional details or select a filter to view specific cuisine types.

What does the “landscape of success” look like amongst different cuisine types?

Breakfast/Brunch and American Cuisine dominate the high star-power buckets — comprising a 46% share of 4-star reviewed restaurants and a 46% share of 5-star reviewed restaurants. Additionally, Japanese food, while not as widespread as American or Breakfast restaurants, is nearly always in the 4-star range. Restaurant owners looking for reviewing success should avoid fast-food style restaurants, which dominate the 2-star category. So where should I open my restaurant?

For those looking to open a new restaurant or open an additional location of an existing restaurant, restaurants closer to the city’s center tend to do better. What other factors should I consider?

The two restaurant qualities having the most direct impact on restaurant performance are outdoor seating and accepting reservations. Restaurants with outdoor seating were far more likely to have higher reviews. So go ahead and open up that patio, sidewalk, or courtyard seating because your customers will thank you! Accepting reservations seems to have a similar effect, where if a restaurant accepts reservations, they are more likely to have high review scores. Not accepting reservations, however, seems to lead to higher check-in numbers, possibly because of the ease of just dropping in. For long-term success, we recommend optimizing for reviews (remember, success begets success), but for a short-term sprint focused on maximizing customers, declining to accept reservations would increase foot traffic.

So how do I optimize my restaurant?

  1. Pay attention to Yelp reviews! Yelp is more relevant and popular than ever, with more reviews written in the past 4 years than in the 12 years before that.
  2. You should aim to get your restaurant in the 4+ star range, which is where we see the biggest boost in “check-in” numbers from ratings.
  3. Offer breakfast/brunch food or American cuisine, as these two categories dominate the high-star tiers. Or consider Japanese cuisine as a safe bet, for most Japanese restaurants sit comfortably around the 4 star range.
  4. If starting a new restaurant, pick a location close to the city center, as higher scoring reviews are concentrated closer to the city center.
  5. Increase your star rating by offering outdoor seating and accepting reservations, either over the phone or through some online platform.
